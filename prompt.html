How-To Guide for Investment Finance

🧠 Run Deep Research with AI Tools

The tools available within ChatGPT for investment workflows now include:

  • Create an image: Visualize concepts, strategies, or charts for presentations or reports.
  • Search the web: Access real-time financial news, macroeconomic data, or company updates.
  • Write or code: Generate Python, SQL, or Markdown scripts for data analysis and reporting.
  • Run deep research: Combine reasoning, summarization, and citations to synthesize in-depth financial insights.

These tools are especially useful for users building reports, conducting valuations, or monitoring key financial signals with up-to-date context.

Choosing the Right GPT Model for Finance

🔍 Use Case 1: Analyzing a Stock

  • Extracting financial ratios from 10-Ks or earnings transcripts
  • Evaluating price performance, volatility, or comparing to benchmarks
  • Writing investment theses or summarizing financial statements

Best Model: GPT-4 Turbo

Why? Fast, low-latency, handles complex documents and longer context.

💬 Use Case 2: Sentiment Analysis

  • Interpret investor tone and comments from forums or social media
  • Summarize bullish/bearish perspectives and sector shifts

Best Model: GPT-4 Turbo or GPT-4

Why? More accurate understanding of nuanced and coded language than GPT-3.5

💼 Use Case 3: Portfolio Screening

  • Screen stocks (e.g. P/E < 15, ROE > 10%)
  • Generate summaries or help draft investment memos

Best Model: GPT-3.5

Why? Fast, cost-effective, suitable for structured tasks but less robust with complex logic

✅ Summary Table

Task Best Model Why
Deep stock analysis GPT-4 Turbo Handles complex financial documents efficiently
Sentiment analysis GPT-4 / Turbo Captures nuance, sarcasm, and context better than GPT-3.5
Simple screening GPT-3.5 Cost-effective for basic structured analysis

Prompting Techniques

1. Clarity & Specificity

  • “Act as an equity analyst with 10 years’ experience.”
  • “List the top 5 ETFs by 5-year annualized return.”

2. Explicit Constraints

  • “Provide a table with columns: Ticker, P/E, Dividend Yield, Expense Ratio.”
  • “Summarize in no more than three bullet points.”

3. Prompt Chaining

  1. “Analyze the revenue growth of AAPL over 5 years.”
  2. “Evaluate its debt-to-equity trend.”
  3. “Based on these, suggest potential risks.”

4. Domain-Specific Adaptations

  • “Using this CSV of 10-year treasury yields, show yield curve changes.”
  • “Compare valuations using DCF assumptions.”

5. Template & Pattern Usage

  • Persona Pattern: “You are a risk manager; identify top portfolio risks.”
  • Template Pattern: “Report format: • Summary • Metrics • Recommendation.”

Good Prompt Examples

  • “Act as a veteran equity analyst and summarize the financial health of TSLA for the past 3 years, focusing on liquidity and profitability ratios.”
  • “Provide a side-by-side comparison table of VOO vs. IVV including expense ratios, average annual returns, and top 3 sector exposures.”
  • “Simulate a risk report: If interest rates rise by 2%, what is the likely impact on long-duration corporate bond ETFs?”
  • “Draft an investment thesis outline for Bitcoin, covering macro catalysts, technical trends, and major risks, in three bullet points each.”
  • “Compare and contrast covered call vs. protective put strategies on AAPL stock, listing pros, cons, and breakeven points.”
